STUDYING IN THE US

A simple but useful guide on resources you need to study in the United States.

Assess Your Eligibility:

Check if you meet the basic requirements to study in the US. This typically includes having a high school certificate (KCSE, A-levels), proficiency in English (You will need to take the TOEFL, IELTS or Duolingo test), and the financial capacity to cover tuition and living expenses. Our page on on financial options covers this.

Standardized Testing:

Many US colleges require SAT or ACT scores for admission but some are test optional. Research the requirements for the institutions you're interested in and start preparing for these tests well in advance.Free resources like Khan Academy, offer free SAT practice.

Research US Colleges and Universities:

Look for colleges and universities that offer programs aligned with your interests and career goals. Consider factors like location, size, coursed (commonly called majors) offered, and available financial aid.

Prepare Necessary Documents:

Organize essential documents such as transcripts (this is a document from your school with all the classes taken and grades aquired each year), recommendation letters from your principal, standardized test scores, and any other certificates required for the application process.

Find Suitable College Application Platforms:

There are multiple platforms where you can apply to US colleges, such as the Common Application or Scoir. Research which colleges use these platforms and create an account accordingly. Scoir is best for students who may have a hard time paying application fees as all the universities on the website allow free applications.

Write a Strong Personal Statement/Essay:

Many colleges require a personal statement or essay as part of the application. Use this opportunity to showcase your uniqueness, experiences, and academic goals. Seek feedback from teachers to make your essay stand out.

Apply to Colleges:

Apply to a mix of reach, match, and safety schools. Reach schools are more competitive, match schools have a good chance of acceptance, and safety schools are those where your academic credentials exceed their requirements.

Stay on Top of Deadlines:

Make a timeline for completing all the necessary application steps and adhere to deadlines strictly.

Prepare for Interviews (if required):

Some colleges may require interviews as part of the application process. Practice common interview questions and be ready to discuss your academic interests and career aspirations.

Stay Positive and Persevere:

The process of applying to US colleges can be challenging, but don't get discouraged. Stay positive and keep working hard to achieve your goals.
